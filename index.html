A Life of Pursuit

Some of Coltrane's most important Albums:

  • 1958 - Blue Train

    Coltrane's debut album was released on the Blue Note Record label. The band included Paul Chambers on bass, Philly Joe Jones on drums, Kenny Drew on piano, Lee Morgan on trumpet and Curtis Fuller on trombone.

  • 1959 - Kind of Blue (as a sideman with Miles Davis)

    One of the most famous jazz records that included; Miles Davis on trumpet, Cannonball Adderley on alto saxophone, Coltrane on tenor saxophone, Bill Evans on piano, Paul Chambers on bass, Jimmy Cobb on drums, and Wynton Kelly on piano for "Freddie Freeloader" and "So What".

  • 1960 - Giant Steps

    Released on Atlantic Records and included one of his most challenging and most famous compositions, Giant Steps.

  • 1960 (10/21 - 10/26) - My Favorite Things; Coltrane Plays the Blues; Coltrane's Sound; Coltrane Jazz

    An incredible few days of recording for Atlantic Records where Coltrane released one of his most recognized arrangement of My Favorite Things on soprano saxophone.

  • 1962 - Ballads

    An album released on Impulse! Records that showed Coltrane's softer side and included beautiful melodic playing of many well known ballads.

  • 1964 - A Love Supreme

    Also released on Impulse! Records, this album is one of Coltrane's bestselling albums and considered to be his masterpiece. The album was Coltrane's embodied his devotion for his faith.

“My music is the spiritual expression of what I am — my faith, my knowledge, my being...When you begin to see the possibilities of music, you desire to do something really good for people, to help humanity free itself from its hangups...I want to speak to their souls.”

― John Coltrane
